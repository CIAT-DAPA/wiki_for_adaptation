Tracking Framework

Interventions vs Impact vs Causal Links

A tracking framework systematically monitors the connections between adaptation interventions, their implementation, and the resulting impacts, ensuring accountability and learning throughout the adaptation process.

Understanding the Framework

The tracking framework consists of three key components:

  • Interventions: The adaptation actions or projects implemented to address climate risks
  • Implementation: How interventions are executed, including activities, outputs, and processes
  • Impacts: The changes resulting from interventions, including outcomes and long-term effects

Establishing Causal Links

Establishing clear causal links between interventions and impacts is essential for:

  • Attributing observed changes to specific adaptation actions
  • Understanding how and why interventions work (or don't work)
  • Improving future adaptation strategies based on evidence
  • Demonstrating accountability to stakeholders and funders
Indicator Framework

Outcome vs Process Indicators

Effective adaptation monitoring requires both outcome indicators (what changed) and process indicators (how change happened), providing a complete picture of adaptation progress.

Outcome Indicators

Outcome indicators measure the actual changes and results achieved through adaptation interventions:

  • Changes in vulnerability or exposure to climate risks
  • Improvements in adaptive capacity
  • Reduced climate-related losses or damages
  • Enhanced resilience of systems, communities, or ecosystems

Process Indicators

Process indicators track how adaptation is being implemented:

  • Number and type of interventions implemented
  • Resources allocated and spent on adaptation
  • Participation and engagement of stakeholders
  • Integration of adaptation into policies and planning

Why Both Are Needed

Using both outcome and process indicators provides:

  • A complete understanding of both implementation progress and actual results
  • Early warning signals when implementation deviates from plans
  • Evidence for adaptive management and course correction
  • Comprehensive reporting for different stakeholder needs
Selection Criteria

Criteria for Selecting Indicators/Metrics

Not all indicators are created equal. Use these criteria to select the most appropriate and effective indicators for your adaptation monitoring needs, with special attention to sensitivity to climate change impacts.

Key Selection Criteria

Relevance

The indicator should directly relate to adaptation goals and the specific climate risks being addressed.

Sensitivity

The indicator should be sensitive to changes in climate conditions and responsive to adaptation interventions.

Measurability

It should be possible to measure the indicator with available resources, data, and technical capacity.

Reliability

Measurements should be consistent, reproducible, and based on credible data sources.

Timeliness

The indicator should provide information when needed for decision-making and adaptive management.

Cost-effectiveness

The benefits of monitoring the indicator should justify the costs of data collection and analysis.

Applying the Criteria

When selecting indicators:

  1. Start with adaptation objectives and climate risks
  2. Identify potential indicators that address these objectives
  3. Evaluate each indicator against the selection criteria
  4. Balance different criteria based on context and priorities
  5. Select a mix of outcome and process indicators
  6. Ensure feasibility within available resources and capacity
