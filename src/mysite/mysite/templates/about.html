About Adaptation Tracking Wiki

Your comprehensive resource for understanding indicators and measurement methods

Our Mission

Adaptation Tracking Wiki serves as a centralized knowledge base for climate scientists, researchers, policymakers, and educators seeking to understand and utilize indicators.

We provide comprehensive documentation on high-level indicators, their associated metrics, measurement methods (methods), and specific observable products (SOPs) that power climate research and monitoring worldwide.

By making this information accessible and well-organized, we aim to accelerate climate research, improve data literacy, and support evidence-based climate action.

Global Coverage

Documentation spans globe climate systems including atmosphere, oceans, cryosphere, and terrestrial ecosystems.

Evidence-Based

All entries are authored by scientists and reviewed to ensure accuracy and scientific rigor.

Community Driven

About This Project

The Adaptation Tracking Wiki was developed as part of the Adaptation Insights project, supported by the Bill & Melinda Gates Foundation.

This initiative aims to enhance understanding of climate adaptation tracking and provide practical tools for assessing adaptation effectiveness. As the wiki grows, we welcome additional partners and contributors to expand its reach and impact.
