Impact Pathways

Project Level vs Policy Level

Impact pathways and indicators differ significantly depending on the scale of intervention. Understanding these differences is crucial for effective monitoring and evaluation.

Project Level Guidance

Best practices for project-scale impact pathways

Focus Areas

  • Clearly defined beneficiaries and target areas
  • Specific, measurable outputs that can be monitored regularly
  • Direct linkages between activities and outcomes
  • Baseline data collection for impact assessment
  • Community participation in indicator selection

Recommended Indicators

  • Number of beneficiaries reached
  • Infrastructure built or improved (units, area, capacity)
  • Training participants and knowledge gained
  • Changes in vulnerability or exposure at local level
  • Adoption rates of adaptation measures

Policy Level Guidance

Best practices for policy-scale impact pathways

Focus Areas

  • Systems-level changes and enabling environment
  • Integration across sectors and governance levels
  • Long-term capacity development and institutional change
  • Policy coherence and mainstreaming metrics
  • National level climate risk and vulnerability trends

Recommended Indicators

  • Number of policies integrating climate adaptation
  • Budget allocations for adaptation across sectors
  • National-level risk reduction metrics
  • Institutional capacity indices
  • Climate Information Integration in planning processes
