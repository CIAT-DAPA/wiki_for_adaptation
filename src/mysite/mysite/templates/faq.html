Frequently Asked Questions

Common questions about the Adaptation Wiki.

What is the difference between an Indicator and a Metric?

An Indicator represents broad climate system changes or phenomena (e.g., Sea Level Rise), while a Metric is a specific quantitative measure used to track that indicator (e.g., Global Mean Sea Level measured in millimeters). One indicator can have multiple metrics measuring different aspects of the same phenomenon.

What are Methods and how do they relate to Metrics?

Methods describe different approaches or techniques for measuring a metric. Each metric can have multiple methods, each with its own advantages, limitations, and use cases. Methods help you choose the most appropriate measurement approach for your specific context.

What are SOPs and why are they important?

SOPs (Standard Operating Procedures) are specific data products with detailed technical specifications for data collection and analysis. They ensure consistency and reproducibility in climate measurements across different projects and organizations. Each SOP provides specifications like units, frequency, geographic scale, and technical capacity requirements.

How is the content organized hierarchically?

The wiki follows a three-level hierarchy: Indicators (broad phenomena) → Metrics (specific measures) → SOPs (data products). This structure helps you navigate from general concepts to specific implementation details, making it easy to find both high-level information and technical specifications.

Who can contribute content to this wiki?

The wiki welcomes contributions from climate scientists, researchers, and practitioners. If you're interested in becoming an editor, visit the "Become an editor" page to learn about requirements and submit an application. All contributions are reviewed by our editorial team to ensure accuracy and scientific rigor.

How often is the content updated?

The wiki is continuously updated as new research emerges and measurement techniques evolve. Each entry shows its publication and last update dates. Our team of editors regularly reviews and updates content to ensure accuracy and incorporate the latest scientific findings.

Can I download or export data from the wiki?

While the wiki provides comprehensive information about indicators, metrics, and SOPs, it does not directly host climate datasets. However, each SOP entry includes references to data sources and available tools where you can access actual data. Some entries also provide links to code repositories and data portals.

What should I do if I find an error or outdated information?

Please use our "Feedback/Corrections" form to report any errors, outdated information, or suggest improvements. Our editorial team reviews all feedback and makes updates as needed. Your input helps us maintain the quality and accuracy of the wiki.
